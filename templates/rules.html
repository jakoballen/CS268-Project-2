Rules:

Mahjong has several special rules to keep track of while playing outside of the ordinary rule set. These typically determine when a reset is required or when a penalty should be issued out.

Swap-calling

Swap-calling (kuikae) is not allowed. It is not allowed to claim a tile and immediately discard the same tile. It is not allowed to claim a tile for chi and discard the tile from other end of the chow.

Tenpai:

A player is in tenpai when they are 1 tile from a complete hand, players not in tenpai by the end of a draw must pay a 1000-3000 point penalty to those who are.

Furiten

If a waiting player can form a hand using one of his previous discards, he is furiten and is not allowed to claim a win on a discard, even if the hand completed with the previous discard is without a han. Players in Furtien are still able to win on a self draw. A player who fails to claim a win on a discard that completes a mahjong hand, is temporarily furiten, even if the passed tile gives him no yaku, and he cannot claim a win on a discard in the current set of turns. The state of temporary furiten ends next time the player draws or claims a tile. However, if the player has declared riichi he remains temporarily furiten until the end of the hand.

Chombo:

Chombo is a penalty payment to all of the other players totaling a mangan value of 8000 points. There are several ways to trigger Chombo. If you are the dealer and commit a penalty the payment is increased to a haneman of 12000 points.

Punishable Offenses:

  1. Invalidly declaring a win and showing the tiles. The win can be invalid because the hand is not a valid winning hand or because the declaration was not valid
  2. Declaring riichi on a hand that is not waiting. This is determined only in case of a drawn game. In the case where a valid riichi hand is only considered noten because it was declared a dead hand after the riichi declaration, it is not considered a chombo, and only the noten penalty is paid
  3. Making an invalid concealed kan after declaring riichi (determined only if the offender declares a win or in case of a drawn game).
  4. Attempting to claim a tile after the hand has been declared a dead hand.
  5. Play cannot continue due to a mistake of a player. E.g. too many tiles were revealed for the game to continue fairly

Dead Hand:

Some irregularities that are not punished by chombo result instead in a dead hand. A player who has a dead hand is not allowed to declare a win, kong, pung or chow, and can never be considered tenpai.

Conditions For Dead Hand:

  1. Invalidly declaring a win without showing the tiles.
  2. Attempting to declare riichi on a open hand.
  3. Attempting to declare riichi, but failing to call “riichi” or failing to rotate the discard.
  4. Too few or too many tiles on the hand.
  5. Making an invalid kong, pung or chow. The mistake can be corrected if it is realized or pointed out before the discard is made.
  6. Swap-calling.
  7. A referee call may result in a dead hand, e.g. for passing information.

Abortive Draws:

Abortive draws are no longer officially supported in tournament play, however many locals still play by these rules. In the event that 5 kans are called in one round then that round is finished after. In the event that every player discards the same wind in a row then end the round. If a player draws 9 different terminal or honor tiles in his opening hand he may declare a reset. If all players declare riichi in the same round reset. Lastly if 3 players declare ron on one discard then reset.
